Beyond Pizza Parties: What Actually Builds Team Cohesion?

By Khadija Demir

Published on May 20, 2025 · 7 min read

Drawing from clinical psychology and organizational behavior, we dissect the myths of team building and present a framework for fostering genuine connection.

The Myth of Team Building Events

While pizza parties and off-site retreats are fun, they rarely address the root causes of team cohesion. True connection is built through trust, vulnerability, and shared purpose. Many organizations invest in surface-level activities, hoping to boost morale, but these efforts often fail to create lasting change. Real cohesion is not about occasional events—it's about the daily experience of belonging, psychological safety, and shared goals.

Research shows that teams with high cohesion outperform others in both productivity and well-being. However, cohesion cannot be "forced" through artificial means. It emerges organically when people feel seen, heard, and valued for their authentic selves. Leaders who focus on building trust and fostering open communication lay the groundwork for genuine connection.

Building Genuine Connection

  • Encourage open dialogue and active listening. Create regular opportunities for team members to share their perspectives and ideas without fear of judgment.
  • Celebrate team wins and learn from failures together. Recognize both individual and collective achievements, and treat setbacks as opportunities for growth.
  • Foster a culture of psychological safety and inclusion. Make it clear that every voice matters, and that diversity of thought is a strength, not a liability.

Additionally, invest in relationship-building outside of formal work tasks. Informal check-ins, mentorship programs, and cross-functional projects can help break down silos and build trust across the organization. Remember, connection is a continuous process, not a one-time event.

A Framework for Lasting Cohesion

Lasting team cohesion is built on a foundation of trust, shared values, and ongoing communication. Leaders must model these behaviors and create space for authentic connection. This means being transparent about challenges, inviting feedback, and showing vulnerability when appropriate.

To sustain cohesion, organizations should regularly assess team climate and address issues proactively. Use surveys, focus groups, and one-on-one conversations to gather insights and identify areas for improvement. By making cohesion a strategic priority, you empower your teams to collaborate, innovate, and achieve extraordinary results—together.
